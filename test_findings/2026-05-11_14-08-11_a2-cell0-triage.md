## Implication for the A.2 floor sweep

- **Floor of 0.5 may be too permissive on this corpus.** All 4 real misses look like vector-pollution, not vector-recall failures. Raising `VEC_SIMILARITY_FLOOR` to 0.60 should drop the weakest vector candidates and let FTS+graph anchors win the rank-key.
- **Risk:** raising the floor on the detailed corpus may also drop the IRL `colloquial_relationship` lift (13/16 at floor=0.5), because that lift came from vector hits in the 0.5–0.7 cosine range. The sweep needs the IRL gate (`colloquial_relationship` ≥10/16 AND IRL total ≥56/68) on every cell, not just on the winner.
- **Cell 0 detailed = 94/110**, so Cell 1 (floor=0.60) needs to flip ≥2 of {81, 86, 87, 94} from MISS to PASS while not losing IRL or non-relationship detailed hits.

## Side note — matcher-as-triage-filter rule, validated

This triage corrected two false matcher-FN calls from the 2026-05-09 spot-check. Both were misjudged because the spot-check looked at "does the answer name *a* memory" rather than "does the answer name *the* memory the question's structural connection (DERIVED_FROM, causal) points to". The matcher-as-triage rule (real misses require Claude-confirmation, not just visual answer-pattern matching) earned its keep here.
